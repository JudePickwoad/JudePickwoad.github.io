Face ID Smart Lock - Electrical Engineering Capstone Project

For my capstone project, I worked with a team of five students: two electrical and three computer engineering students to design and develop a contactless smart lock with facial recognition as its primary security feature. Over two semesters, we researched and developed a solution tailored for homeowners with mobility and dexterity challenges. The lock ensures ease of access without physical keys or PIN codes.

As the CAD designer, I was responsible for designing the lock‚Äôs outer shell and optimizing it for functionality and aesthetics. I created two alternate designs, and the final prototype was 3D-printed using plastic to test its feasibility. The smart lock used a Raspberry Pi and Raspberry Pi Camera, that provided real-time facial recognition processing. It was designed to be battery-operated, making it wireless and easy to install. Additionally, we developed a web-based companion app that allowed users to manage access remotely, adding an extra layer of convenience. For added security and emergency access, the system also featured a deadbolt lock that could be opened with a physical key.

This project was a defining experience in my engineering education, strengthening my skills in hardware integration, CAD modelling, and collaborative problem-solving while applying cutting-edge technology to real-world accessibility challenges.

Pet Weather Monoriting System

Newfoundland‚Äôs harsh winters and unpredictable weather challenge pet owners, often limiting outdoor activities and posing safety risks. Conventional weather apps provide generalized forecasts that may not account for localized microclimates, making it difficult to determine safe conditions for pet walks. To address this, my partner and I developed a personalized weather data logger that provides real-time, location-specific weather insights tailored to pet owners' needs.

The system integrates sensors to measure temperature, humidity, and rainfall, offering pet owners accurate weather data to assess the safety of outdoor conditions. Data logging capabilities allow users to track historical weather patterns, identify optimal walking windows and mitigate risks associated with extreme conditions. Signal processing techniques ensure that sensor readings are accurate and free from noise, enhancing the reliability of the data.

Once processed, the weather data is transmitted via Bluetooth to a companion mobile app, allowing pet owners to monitor conditions and easily receive recommendations for pet-friendly outdoor activities. This system not only improves pet safety but also fosters community engagement, enabling owners to share insights and best practices for caring for pets during inclement weather. By leveraging advanced sensor technology and data analytics, this innovative solution empowers pet owners to make informed decisions, ensuring their pets remain active and healthy despite Newfoundland‚Äôs challenging climate.

Snow and Weather Monitoring System

To test and refine my circuit design and CAD skills, I designed a weather measuring system using SketchUp 3D before bringing it to life as a functional prototype. BATSUB Renovation Project

During my work term, I was tasked with creating a detailed floor plan and mapping plumbing lines for the BATSUB (British Army Training and Support Unit Belize) bathroom renovation project. Using AutoCAD, I developed an accurate and comprehensive layout, ensuring that the plumbing system was efficiently designed to meet the facility's needs. During discussions with the client, I identified a recurring issue: excessive dirt being tracked into the facilities and proposed a practical solution by recommending the installation of two outdoor faucets for washing dirty equipment. To better illustrate my recommendation, I used SketchUp 3D to visualize the placement of the faucets within the renovation plan. This project strengthened my technical drafting skills, enhanced my ability to assess client needs, and reinforced the importance of problem-solving through design modifications.

3D Design Projects

As part of my Engineering 1030 coursework, I worked on two impactful design projects. These projects reinforced my problem-solving skills and introduced me to computer-aided design (CAD) tools like SolidWorks.

The first project focused on creating an eco-friendly alternative to traditional tabletop preschool toys. Inspired by a pinball machine, the design emphasized sustainability. It was made from 90% recycled materials, significantly reducing the manufacturing process's carbon footprint. Beyond its environmental benefits, the toy was designed to be interactive and educational, encouraging the development of rotary skills through an engaging, puzzle-like assembly process. This hands-on feature not only enhanced user involvement but also aligned with STEM learning principles, making it both fun and functional.

For the second project, my team and I tackled a critical real-world issue: designing a water-carrying system for African isolated communities. After evaluating multiple concepts, we selected a drum cart system, optimizing it for efficiency, durability, and ease of transport. Each component was first modelled in SolidWorks, allowing us to refine the design before assembly. This project was a pivotal moment in my engineering education, as it provided me with practical experience in CAD modelling, collaborative problem-solving, and human-centred design, reinforcing my passion for creating solutions that have a tangible impact.

House Floor Plan

I revisited SketchUp 3D to develop a detailed 3D model of a floor plan, refining my skills in architectural visualization. I began by sourcing a general floor plan online and accurately scaling it to maintain correct proportions. Using this as a guide, I traced the layout and built the structure, incorporating essential architectural elements such as walls, windows, and doors.

To enhance realism, I applied appropriate textures for floors and walls, ensuring a visually appealing and functional design. Additionally, I imported 3D assets, including furniture and fixtures, to create a more immersive and lifelike representation of the space.

This project allowed me to strengthen my proficiency in SketchUp, improve my spatial awareness, and refine my ability to translate 2D designs into fully realized 3D environments.
